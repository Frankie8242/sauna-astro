Why not time?

Two people spending 15 minutes in the same sauna will reach completely different core temperatures — depending on fitness, resting heart rate, and acclimatisation.

Time is a guess. Core temperature is the data.

Peer-Reviewed Research

The data behind it.

Every zone threshold is grounded in published research. Not wellness trends. Not guesswork.

Cardiovascular Protection

Regular sauna use at sufficient core temperatures reduces risk of sudden cardiac death by up to 63% in the largest longitudinal study of its kind.

Laukkanen et al. · JAMA Internal Medicine · 2018

Growth Hormone Surge

Heat stress triggers a growth hormone surge of 2–5× baseline. The response is dose-dependent — more pronounced at higher core temperatures.

Casanueva et al.

Heat Shock Proteins

HSP70 and HSP90 activate when core temperature crosses ~38°C. These molecular chaperones repair damaged proteins and reduce inflammation.

Hussain et al. · Journal of Applied Physiology · 2019

BDNF — Brain Growth Factor

Brain-derived neurotrophic factor rises significantly with heat exposure. Linked to improved mood, neuroplasticity, and cognitive protection.

Rhonda Patrick · FoundMyFitness

Cortisol Reduction

Post-session cortisol drops significantly. Heat activates the parasympathetic nervous system — the same pathway triggered by meditation.

Laukkanen et al. · Kukkonen-Harjula et al.

Norepinephrine Spike

Norepinephrine spikes up to 3× during heat exposure — sharpening focus and mood. The effect extends well beyond the session itself.

Srámek et al. · Journal of Applied Physiology · 2000
